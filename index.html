When choosing software provider — are you asking yourself questions such as:

  • Are they able to develop my software succesfully?
  • Can and will they prevent delays and develop on time?
  • Is this going to be expensive?

How to hire the right software company?

Do they understand my problem?

They must be able to understand your problem first. It’s much more important to know that you’re solving the right problem instead of solving the problem right.

Are they good match for our company culture?

To make sure they’re good fit — test them out with small project that they have to deliver in short period of time — or let them work on smaller tasks so you can test their compatibility with your company.

No interviews or friendly introductions will prevent you from picking up the wrong company. Only real work will do.

Are they actively preventing delays?

The best way to prevent delays is to work in short iterations, usually one week. Each iteration should be a small milestone with planning and outcomes delivered at the end of the iteration.

How to avoid spending fortune?

Fix the time, resources (budget, people) and keep flexible scope. Always focus on the scope the most.
